ESOPHAGOGASTRODUODENOSCOPY DATE: 02/09/2006

PATIENT:

Delphine Brown


Primary Care Physician: Dr. Singleton


PREOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS (indication): Epigastric Pain and Severe GERD

POSTOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS: Hiatal Hernia and Gastritis

PREOPERATIVE PHYSICAL: Physical was unremarkable as documented.

INFORMED CONSENT: Risks, benefits, alternatives and conscious sedation issues were discussed with patient prior to the procedure, including the risks (bleeding, infection, perforation and/or missing polyps). Appropriate informed consent was obtained and signed by the physician and the responsible party.


PREOPERATIVE MEDICATIONS: 50mg Demerol slow IV push and 4mg Versed slow IV push in 1 mg increments

PREOPERATIVE ANTIBIOTICS: none


INTRA-OPERATIVE MEDICATIONS: none


SPECIMEN: 2


PROCEDURE: The patient was placed in the high Fowler's position with the head elevated at a 90° angle. The patient's throat was sprayed twice with Cetacaine and a bite block was applied to keep the mouth open for the scope insertion. Using Olympus videoscope the patient's mouth was intubated. No lesions were noticed in the vocal cords. Scope tip was gradually advanced in the esophagus to the esophagogastral junction, noted at 35 cm from the incisors. A 5cm hiatal hernia was noted. Tongue of gastric tissue slightly less than 1cm noted at the distal esophagus. Advancement of the scope was continued towards the stomach entering through the lower esophageal sphincter (LES) and following the lesser curvature to view the pylorus. Scope was advanced through the pyloric sphincter to view the duodenal bulb and the second part of the duodenum which appeared normal. The scope was then withdrawn back into the stomach to view the antrum where gastritis was noted. Two cold biopsies which appeared red and inflammed were taken in the antrum to rule out H. pylori. The scope then advanced to view the body and fundus. The scope tip was then retroflexed to view the LES. The scope was then removed from the patient, thus concluding the procedure.


IMPRESSIONS:

Severe Gastritis, 5cm Hiatal Hernia


RECOMMENDATIONS:

Follow up with EGD in 5 years to rule out Barretts.


PATHOLOGY:

Stomach, Antrum Biopsy:

Chronic Gastritis
