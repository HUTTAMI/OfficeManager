DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE: Patient was placed in the left lateral decubitus position. Using Olympus videoscope, the patient's rectum was intubated after lubricating anal area. No significant lesions were noticed in the perineum. External Hemorrhoids noted. Scope tip was then gradually advanced up to the cecum, which was identified by landmarks including converging tinea coli, appendiceal orifice and the bulbous ileocecal valve. The appendical orifice was photodocumented. Scope tip was gradually withdrawn while circumferentially examining the luminal surfaces of the ascending, transverse descending and sigmoid colon. Numerous large diverticulae seen on the right colon.


A 5-6 mm polyp that was raised, red and sessile was encountered at 15 cm in the recto-sigmoid and transected by snare polypectomy. Polyp was destroyed.


A 4 cm polyp that was pedunculated, long pale stalk, oblong and with pink head was encountered at 15 cm in the recto-sigmoid and transected by snare polypectomy. Polyp was destroyed.


The scope tip was retroflexed in the rectum. Internal hemorrhoids were seen. Anal Papillae were not seen. The scope tip unretroflexed and insuflated air was suctioned and the scope tip was then withdrawn from the patient, thus concluding the procedure. Melanosis Coli was not noted. Lipoma was not noted. Patient tolerated the procedure well. Patient was given post-endoscopy instruction.


Prep was considered fair.
